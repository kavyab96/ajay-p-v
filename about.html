About Me

I am a passionate material Science researcher committed to advancing sustainability through polymer science, wastewater treatment, and bioinspired material innovation. Over the years, my academic and professional journey has taken me through diverse domains, from biodegradable packaging to heavy metal remediation and high-performance composites for 3D printing.

My early research began at IIT Guwahati as a Project Intern, where I worked on polyhydroxybutyrate-based antimicrobial packaging films-an experience that sparked my lasting interest in biopolymers. I continued at IIT Guwahati as a Junior Research Fellow, contributing to a government-funded project on membrane-assisted bioprocesses for recovering heavy metals from acid mine drainage.

In 2021, I joined CIPET-LARPM Bhubaneswar as a Project Associate, focusing on the development of next-generation biomedical devices, particularly those leveraging polymeric and hybrid materials.

Currently, I am pursuing my Ph.D. at the Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD), as part of the Bioinspired & Integrative Science Lab (The Fermart Lab). Here, I develop novel materials and manufacturing techniques inspired by biological systems. My research integrates additive manufacturing, microelectronics, and sustainable composites with a clear focus on real-world impact.
